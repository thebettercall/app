Part 1: Schengen vs. Non-Schengen

Understanding the difference is vital because a Schengen visa does not grant entry to all European countries.

The Schengen Area is a zone of 29 European countries that have abolished internal borders.

The 29 Countries: Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria*, Croatia, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Italy, Latvia, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Netherlands, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania*, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, and Switzerland.

*Bulgaria and Romania joined in March 2024 (air/sea borders only).

Part 2: Types of European Visas

Type A Airport Transit Visa

For travelers connecting through a Schengen airport to a non-Schengen destination. You must stay in the transit zone.

Type C Short-Stay Visa

Tourists, business, or family visits. Allows stay of up to 90 days within any 180-day period.

Type LTV Limited Territorial Validity

Valid ONLY for the specific issuing country. Rare cases (humanitarian, emergencies).

Type D National Long-Stay Visa

For living, working, or studying (>90 days). Allows travel around Schengen for up to 90 days.

Part 7: Police Clearance Certificate (PCC)

What is it? Official document enumerating criminal records.

When needed? Mandatory for Type D Visas. Usually not for tourist visas.

Which countries? Any country where you lived >6-12 months in the past 3-10 years.

Must be recent (issued within last 3-6 months) and often Apostilled.

Part 8: Document Verification (Apostille)

A stamp verifying the authenticity of a document for use in Hague Convention countries. It replaces complex "Legalization".
Colloquial term for Electronic Register verification used by officers to check if an Apostille is genuine online.

Part 12: TRP vs. TRC

TRP (Permit)

The Legal Status / Decision granting you the right to stay.

TRC (Card)

The Physical Document (plastic card). Allows travel in Schengen.

Part 13: Visa Rejection

Main Causes:

  • Insufficient Funds / Lump Sum deposits.
  • Unclear Purpose or Vague Itinerary.
  • Lack of ties to home country.
  • SIS Security Alert.

Solutions:

Appeal (within 1 month) or Reapply with corrected documents.

Part 18: Indian Applicants (ECR)

ECR Emigration Check Required. No 10th grade certificate.

Europe is ECNR: You do NOT need POE clearance to work in Europe, even with an ECR passport.

Part 19: Drivers (Code 95)

Code 95 is mandatory CPC certification for driving commercial vehicles >3.5t in EU.

Foreign licenses aren't enough. Drivers usually get a work visa specifically to take the Code 95 course upon arrival.

Part 20: Netto vs. Brutto

Brutto
Gross Salary

Contract value BEFORE taxes. Includes social security (30-45%).

Netto
In Hand

Actual take-home money. Always negotiate in Netto.
